Hatred/Anger

Hatred stirs up quarrels, but love makes up for all offenses. (10:12)

Hiding Hatred makes you a liar; slandering others makes you a fool. (10:18)

People with understanding control their anger; a hot temper shows great foolishness. (14:29)

A gentle answer deflects anger, but harsh words make tempers flare. (15:1)

A hot-tempered person starts fights; a cool-tempered person stops them. (15:18)

The anger of the king is a deadly threat; the wise will try to appease it. (16:14)

Starting a quarrel is like opening a floodgate, so stop before a dispute breaks out. (17:14)

Anyone who loves to quarrel loves sin; anyone who trusts in high walls invites disaster. (17:19)
