Leadership

Without wise leadership, a nation falls; there is safety in having many advisers. (11:14)

Work hard and become a leader; be lazy and become a slave. (12:24)

Those who spare the rod of discipline hate their children. Those who love their children care enough to discipline them. (13:24)

A growing population is a king's glory; a prince without subjects has nothing. (14:28)

Godliness makes a nation great, but sin is a disgrace to any people. (14:34)

A king rejoices in wise servants but is angry with those who disgrace him. (14:35)

Plans go wrong for lack of advice; many advisers bring success. (15:22)

The king speaks with divine wisdom; he must never judge unfairly. (16:10)

A king detests wrongdoing, for his rule is built on justice. (16:12)

The king is pleased with words from righteous lips; he loves those who speak honestly. (16:13)

The anger of the king is a deadly threat; the wise will try to appease it. (16:14)

When the king smiles, there is life; his favor refreshes like a spring rain. (16:15)

A wise servant will rule over the master's disgraceful son and will share the inheritance of the master's children. (17:2)

It is wrong to punish the godly for being good or to flog leaders for being honest. (17:26)
