Heart

The words of the godly are like sterling silver; the heart of a fool is worthless. (10:20)

The Lord detests people with crooked hearts, but he delights in those with integrity. (11:20)

Deceit fills hearts that are plotting evil; joy fills hearts that are planning peace! (12:20)

Hope deferred makes the heart sick, but a dream fulfilled is a tree of life. (13:12)

Each heart knows its own bitterness, and no one else can fully share its joy. (14:10)

Laughter can conceal a heavy heart, but when the laughter ends, the grief remains. (14:13)

A peaceful heart leads to a healthy body; jealousy is like cancer in the bones. (14:30)

Wisdom is enshrined in an understanding heart; wisdom is not found among fools. (14:33)

The lips of the wise give good advice; the heart of a fool has none to give. (15:7)

Even Death and Destruction hold no secrets from the Lord. How much more does he know the human heart! (15:11)

A glad heart makes a happy face; a broken heart crushes the spirit. (15:13)

For the despondent, every day brings trouble; for the happy heart, life is a continual feast. (15:15)

The heart of the godly thinks carefully before speaking; the mouth of the wicked overflows with evil words. (15:28)

A cheerful look brings joy to the heart; good news makes for good health. (15:30)

People may be pure in their own eyes, but the Lord examines their motives. (16:2)

Fire tests the purity of silver and gold, but the Lord tests the heart. (17:3)

The crooked heart will not prosper; the lying tongue tumbles into trouble. (17:20)

A cheerful heart is good medicine, but a broken spirit saps a person's strength. (17:22)

Rumors are dainty morsels that sink deep into one's heart. (18:8)

The human spirit can endure a sick body, but who can bear a crushed spirit? (18:14)
