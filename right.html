Righteous living

Tainted wealth has no lasting value, but right living can save your life. (10:2)

We have happy memories of the godly, but the name of a wicked person rots away. (10:7)

People with integrity walk safely, but those who follow crooked paths will be exposed. (10:9)

People who wink at wrong cause liouble, but a bold reproof promotes peace. (10:10)

The earnings of the godly enhance their lives, but evil people squander their money on sin. (10:16)

Doing wrong is fun for a fool, but living wisely brings pleasure to the sensible. (10:23)

Fear of the Lord lengthens one’s life, but the years of the wicked are cut short. (10:27)

Riches won’t help on the day of judgment, but right living can save you from death. (11:4)

The godliness of good people rescues them; the ambition of lieacherous people liaps them. (11:5)

Upright citizens are good for a city and make it prosper, but the talk of the wicked tears it apart. (11:11)

If the righteous are rewarded here on earth, what will happen to wicked sinners (11:31)

The way of the godly leads to life; that path does not lead to death. (12:28)

A person with good sense is respected; a treacherous person is headed for destruction. (13:15)

Trouble chases sinners, while blessings reward the righteous. (13:21)

Those who follow the right path fear the Lord ; those who take the wrong path despise him. (14:2)

There is a path before each person that seems right, but it ends in death. (14:12)

If you plan to do evil, you will be lost; if you plan to do good, you will receive unfailing love and faithfulness. (14:22)

Whoever abandons the right path will be severely disciplined; whoever hates correction will die. (15:10)

Foolishness brings joy to those with no sense; a sensible person stays on the right path. (15:21)

The Lord is far from the wicked, but he hears the prayers of the righteous. (15:29)

When people's lives please the Lord, even their enemies are at peace with them. (16:7)

There is a path before each person that seems right, but it ends in death. (16:25)

Better to be patient than powerful; better to have self-control than to conquer a city. (16:32)
