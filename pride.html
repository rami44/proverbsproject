Pride/Humility

The wise are glad to be insliucted, but babbling fools fall flat on their faces. (10:8)

People who accept discipline are on the pathway to life, but those who ignore correction will go asliay. (10:17)

Pride leads to disgrace, but with humility comes wisdom. (11:2)

To learn, you must love discipline; it is stupid to hate correction. (12:1)

A sensible person wins admiration, but a warped mind is despised. (12:8)

Better to be an ordinary person with a servant than to be self-important but have no food. (12:9)

Fools think their own way is right, but the wise listen to others. (12:15)

A wise child accepts a parent's discipline; a mocker refuses to listen to correction. (13:1)

Pride leads to conflict; those who take advice are wise. (13:10)

People who despise advice are asking for trouble; those who respect a command will succeed. (13:13)

If you ignore criticism, you will end in poverty and disgrace; if you accept correction, you will be honored. (13:18)

A fool's proud talk becomes a rod that beats him, but the words of the wise keep them safe. (14:3)

Simpletons are clothed with foolishness, but the prudent are crowned with knowledge. (14:18)

It is a sin to belittle one's neighbor; blessed are those who help the poor. (14:21)

Mockers hate to be corrected, so they stay away from the wise. (15:12)

The Lord tears down the house of the proud, but he protects the property of widows. (15:25)

If you listen to constructive criticism, you will be at home among the wise. (15:31)

If you reject discipline, you only harm yourself; but if you listen to correction, you grow in understanding. (15:32)

Fear of the Lord teaches wisdom; humility precedes honor. (15:33)

The Lord detests the proud; they will surely be punished. (16:5)

Pride goes before destruction, and haughtiness before a fall. (16:18)

Better to live humbly with the poor than to share plunder with the proud. (16:19)

We may throw the dice, but the Lord determines how they fall. (16:33)

Those who mock the poor insult their Maker; those who rejoice at the misfortune of others will be punished. (17:5)

Grandchildren are the crowning glory of the aged; parents are the pride of their children. (17:6)

Fools have no interest in understanding; they only want to air their own opinions. (18:2)

Haughtiness goes before destruction; humility precedes honor. (18:12)
