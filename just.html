Justice

The fears of the wicked will be fulfilled; the hopes of the godly will be granted. (10:24)

When the storms of life come, the wicked are whirled away, but the godly have a lasting foundation. (10:25)

The hopes of the godly result in happiness, but the expectations of the wicked come to nothing. (10:28)

The godly will never be disturbed, but the wicked will be removed from the land. (10:30)

An honest witness tells the truth; a false witness tells lies. (12:17)

A poor person's farm may produce much food, but injustice sweeps it all away. (13:23)

The godly eat to their hearts' content, but the belly of the wicked goes hungry. (13:25)

Backsliders get what they deserve; good people receive their reward. (14:14)

The king speaks with divine wisdom; he must never judge unfairly. (16:10)

A king detests wrongdoing, for his rule is built on justice. (16:12)

Acquitting the guilty and condemning the innocent- both are detestable to the Lord. (17:15)

The wicked take secret bribes to pervert the course of justice. (17:23)

It is not right to acquit the guilty or deny justice to the innocent. (18:5)

The first to speak in court sounds right- until the cross-examination begins. (18:17)
