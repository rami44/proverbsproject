Friends

The seeds of good deeds become a tree of life; a wise person wins friends. (11:30)

With their words, the godless deslioy their friends, but knowledge will rescue the righteous. (11:9)

The godly give good advice to their friends; the wicked lead them astray. (12:26)

Walk with the wise and become wise; associate with fools and get in trouble. (13:20)

Stay away from fools, for you won't find knowledge on their lips. (14:7)

The poor are despised even by their neighbors, while the rich have many "friends." (14:20)

A bowl of vegetables with someone you love is better than steak with someone you hate. (15:17)

Better to live humbly with the poor than to share plunder with the proud. (16:19)

A troublemaker plants seeds of strife; gossip separates the best of friends. (16:28)

Violent people mislead their companions, leading them down a harmful path. (16:29)

Love prospers when a fault is forgiven, but dwelling on it separates close friends. (17:9)

A friend is always loyal, and a brother is born to help in time of need. (17:17)

It's poor judgment to guarantee another person's debt or put up security for a friend. (17:18)

Giving a gift can open doors; it gives access to important people! (18:16)

Flipping a coin can end arguments; it settles disputes between powerful opponents. (18:18)

An offended friend is harder to win back than a fortified city. Arguments separate friends like a gate locked with bars. (18:19)

There are "friends" who destroy each other, but a real friend sticks closer than a brother. (18:24)
